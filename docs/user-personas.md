# User Personas

## Purpose
These personas define who ChromaOS CRM is being built for. Because this is an internal system, the personas are operational users rather than external customers.

---

## Persona 1: The Owner-Operator

### Snapshot
**Name:** Eric  
**Role:** Founder / Operator  
**Primary Need:** Unified visibility and control across all brands

### Goals
- see all leads across all brands in one place
- know what stage every opportunity is in
- know what needs attention now
- route leads correctly
- avoid losing track of next actions
- use AI to reduce repetitive work

### Pain Points
- scattered lead notes
- fragmented workflow across brands
- too much context switching
- sales and proposal prep taking too long
- no single operational dashboard

### What He Needs From The CRM
- clear dashboard
- fast lead lookup
- easy stage movement
- task visibility
- AI actions in context
- multi-brand filtering
- reliable deal tracking

### Success Looks Like
He can open the CRM and know in minutes:
- what came in
- what brand it belongs to
- what needs a response
- what is moving
- what is stuck

---

## Persona 2: The Future Internal Operator

### Snapshot
**Name:** Future Sales / Ops Support  
**Role:** Internal operator or assistant  
**Primary Need:** Clear, structured workflows

### Goals
- manage follow-up cleanly
- update records consistently
- avoid guessing where things belong
- help move leads and deals without confusion

### Pain Points
- unclear process
- weak handoff context
- inconsistent naming or data structure
- no easy way to see what happened before

### What They Need From The CRM
- clear pipeline logic
- clear task ownership
- visible activity history
- easy forms and updates
- fast search and filtering

### Success Looks Like
They can operate the system without needing constant clarification from the owner.

---

## Persona 3: The AI-Assisted Operator

### Snapshot
**Name:** OpenClaw Agent Layer  
**Role:** Embedded assistant system  
**Primary Need:** Structured context and bounded actions

### Goals
- classify leads
- summarize websites or records
- recommend next steps
- generate scoped notes
- save useful outputs back into CRM

### Pain Points
- messy or incomplete record structure
- inconsistent fields
- missing context
- unclear routing logic

### What It Needs From The CRM
- predictable record structure
- clean brand and stage references
- linked lead/company/deal context
- a clear place to store outputs

### Success Looks Like
Agent actions feel useful, repeatable, and grounded in real CRM state rather than isolated prompts.

---

## Shared User Needs

All users need the system to be:
- fast
- clear
- structured
- searchable
- reliable
- multi-brand aware
- operationally useful

---

## Product Implication
The CRM should be optimized first for:
1. owner clarity
2. operational consistency
3. agent-assisted leverage

Not for public polish, vanity dashboards, or unnecessary complexity.\n
